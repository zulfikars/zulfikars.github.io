Biography

I was born in Pati, a small regency in Indonesia (its beauty can be seen here). My academic journey began at Qatar University, where I did my B.A. in International Affairs. I graduated in only three years as one of the best graduates of my batch and I received an award from the Emir of Qatar (my graduation was reported by media in English and in Bahasa Indonesia). During my time in Qatar, I was exposed to various works and scholars on Middle East politics, Islamic politics, and Islamic political thought. It was during this time that I became interested in the growing Chinese influence in the Middle East, which is my primary academic interest, due to not only scholarly exposure but also developments that I witnessed with my own eyes. My undergraduate thesis investigated China’s foreign policy towards the Arab-Israeli conflict since 1949 to 2013. The main finding of this thesis has been converted to an op-ed.

I then went on to pursue an M.A. in International Politics at the Department of Politics, the University of Manchester, UK. At Manchester, whilst maintaining my interests on China-Middle East relations, I also had the opportunity to expand my knowledge on European politics by taking courses on European Union policy making and foreign policy. My M.A. dissertation entitled “A Comparative Study of China’s Approach towards Qatar and the United Arab Emirates” under the supervision of Dr. Elena Barabantseva, received a distinction. After completing my masters, I decided to stay in Manchester to pursue a Ph.D, which I am still currently trying to complete. My thesis examines the implementation of China’s Belt and Road Initiatives and the Asian Investment Infrastructure Bank in the Gulf through a neo-Gramscian perspective. This Ph.D is supervised by Dr. Elena and Dr. Stuart Shields, a critical theory scholar.

Beyond academia, I am an independent journalist and I have published over 150 articles in various media publications around the world (See below for my articles). Despite my academic background, I prefer to write journalistic pieces because they are easily accessible to everyone, unlike academic journals or books which can be accessed by certain few (I elaborate this reason further here). Thus, to this date (or perhaps in the near future), I do not have any journal publications, although I have published one book chapter (See below). My journalistic works focus on Indonesian politics, disability issues, and humanitarian affairs. As I elaborate here, I am working to dedicate my journalistic skills to give the voices to those neglected in the society. Besides Indonesia, I have experiences in reporting from the UK, Slovakia, Norway, Bahrain, and Qatar.

Outside academia and journalism, I am interested in computer sciences, particularly open source software and cyber security. I am also learning to code C++, Python, and Pascal. I never take a formal learning or certification on these subjects, even though I did complete a short roboting programming course at Carnegie Mellon University in Qatar.

I am a disabled. I am unable to use my hands to perform certain actions including handwriting. I have written my story as a disabled in my book in Bahasa Indonesia. I have also written a short summary of the book in Bahasa Indonesia and English. I have been invited to speak about my story as disabled; for example, see here. I have a specific interest on Islam and Disability. I have written two articles on that subject here and here, and I have spoken about the topic at some universities in Indonesia and the UK; for example, see here. In early 2017, I founded Sekolabilitas, an Indonesia-based NGO focusing on helping students with disabilities in accessing proper education (If you know anyone who need help or you would like to get involved, check the website or contact me).

In my free time, I travel the world and hike mountains. My photo above was taken at Ben Nevis, the highest mountain in the British Isles.
