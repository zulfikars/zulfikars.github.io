Dr. Muhammad Zulfikar Rakhmat

I am a researcher from Indonesia. I received my B.A. in International Affairs, with a focus on Middle East and Islamic politics, from Qatar University. This was further shaped by an M.A. in International Politics and a Ph.D in Politics from the University of Manchester in the UK, which I completed at the age of 26.

I am currently Director of the China-Indonesia Desk at the Center of Economic and Law Studies (CELIOS) in Jakarta.

I also hold affiliated positions at the Middle East Institute at the National University of Singapore and Busan University of Foreign Studies. Between 2022 and 2024, I was an associate at the London School of Economics' IDEAS.

Previously, I served as an assistant professor at Universitas Islam Indonesia and as a research associate at Jakarta-based Institute for Development of Economics and Finance (INDEF). I have taught at Universitas Gadjah Mada, Universitasa Airlangga, Bina Nusantara University, Universitas Paramadina, and Universitas Kristen Indonesia.

Additionally, I have have undertaken consulting assignments for a range of organisations, including Oxfam, USAID, Wikistrat, Gulf State Analytics, Freedom House, Indonesia's Ministry of Finance, Grapevine Asia Partners, Doublethink Lab, Dialectica, among others. I am also regularly quoted in leading newspapers, including New York Times, South China Morning Post, Nikkei Asia Review, Voice of America, Al Jazeera, Radio Free Asia, and others.

I received the 'Outstanding Research' award from Qatar National Research Fund in 2015 and the 'Most Productive Author' award from The Conversation Indonesia in 2020 and 2022.

My areas of expertise lie in the political economy of international cooperation, with a particular emphasis on China-Indonesia relations, China-Middle East relations, and Indonesia-Middle East relations.

Nonetheless, between 2014-2019, I was involved in a writing project focusing on Muslim affairs around the world, while from 2015 to 2020, I took part in a project concentrating on socio-economic issues in Indonesia. These projects were funded by Qatar and UK-based institutions respectively. The outputs of these projects, in the form of media articles, are listed in this site.

As a disabled, I am also interested in disability-related issues. Besides writing on this topic, I lead Sekolabilitas, an Indonesian-based NGO dedicated to assist children with disabilities in accessing proper education. I have written my story as a disabled in my book in Indonesian. I have also written a short summary of the book in Indonesian and in English.

From 2020, however, I had decided to only write, accept interviews, or involve in projects related to my core research interests.

In my free time, I enjoy hiking and computer programming.

I am available for commissioned (and sometimes voluntary) works, research projects, and public speaking. You are welcome to email me at muhzulfikar AT gmail DOT com.

In terms of social media, I only use LinkedIn, Instagram, X, and Medium.
